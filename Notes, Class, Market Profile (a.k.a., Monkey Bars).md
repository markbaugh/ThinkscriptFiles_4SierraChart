#### MonkeyBars Area Setup:
1. Anchor High to Low
2. Identify midpoint of 50%, If Fair Price is at 50%, this is Normal Distribution and no adjustments are needed. If Fair Price is Away from 50%, there is Skew to be measured.
3. Measure difference between Fair Price and 50% level.
4. Apply 2Xs difference above the high if there is skew and the skew is bullish. Apply 2Xs difference below if there is skew and the skew is bearish.
5. Re-anchor low to 50% and to new projected high for bullish. Re-anchor low to 50% to new projected low for bearish...

#### Fibonacci Retracement Levels:
0%, 20%, 50%, 80%, 100%, -20%, -20%, -20%, -50%, -80%, -100%, -120%, -150%, -180%, -200%,
0% and 100% are the anchors for the (old) high and low, and the (new) projected high and low.
#### Fibonacci Extension (Measuring tool) Setup:
Though the Fibonacci Extension can be used for other set ups. The only values needed for the setup is the 0% and the 200%.

IMPORTANT: Any product that trades overnight with sufficient liquidity in multiple exchanges use overnight price action. This can also be done for ETF products like SPY, QQQ, IWM, etc. However, for individual equities like AAPL, NVDA, etc ONLY use the daily 09:30-4:00 price action.

2024-09-10: Pivot Point for Monkey Bars is H+L+C/3 of the Daily Candle (High+Low+Close/3). The Pivot Point can be treated like a moving average on the Monkey Bars. Close above the Pivot is Bullish. Close below the Pivot is Bearish. For Pivot Points see Blake Young #TheoTRADE Chat Room 2024-09-10.
